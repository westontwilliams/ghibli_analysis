1. HIDDEN VILLAGE OF THE EMISHI, NORTHEAST JAPAN, IN THE MUROMACHI ERA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Ashitaka rides his mount, Yakkuru, along a forest path towards the village.]

ASHITAKA:
  Yakkuru!

[He meets three village girls in a field.]

KAYA:
  Ani-sama!

ASHITAKA:
  Hii-sama is calling for everyone to return to the village.

KAYA:
  Jii-ji also says so.

ASHITAKA:
  Jii-ji?!

KAYA:
  He said that the mountains are strange......

GIRL B:
  The birds have all disappeared.

GIRL A:
  And all the creatures......

ASHITAKA:
  I see.
  I'm going to check on Jii-ji.
  Everyone hurry back to the village!

THE THREE GIRLS:
  Yes sir!

[Ashitaka climbs a lookout tower near the village, where Jii-ji is keeping watch.]

ASHITAKA:
  Something's coming......
  Jii-ji, what can it be?

YAMA-OJI (AKA JII-JI):
  I don't know. It's not human.

ASHITAKA:
  Back at the village, Hii-sama is calling for everyone to return.

YAMA-OJI:
  It comes......
  It's a Tatari-gami!

ASHITAKA:
  Tatari-gami?!
  Yakkuru, run!!

[The Tatari-gami ("Curse God") knocks down the watchtower. Ashitaka grabs Jii-ji
and leaps with him into the nearest treetop.]

ASHITAKA:
  It's headed towards the village!!
  It's going to attack!

YAMA-OJI:
  Ashitaka,
  Don't touch the Tatari-gami.
  You'll be cursed if you do!!

ASHITAKA:
  Yakkuru!

[Ashitaka rides Yakkuru in front of the Tatari-gami.]

ASHITAKA:
  Calm! Calm down!
  Surely you are to all appearances a mountain god of name,
  but why do you rampage like this?

[The Tatari-gami sees the three girls, and turns towards them.]

GIRL B:
  Monster!

KAYA:
  To the village!

ASHITAKA:
  Stop! Why do you attack our village?!
  Stop it! Calm down!

[Girl B slips and falls; Kaya draws her sword and prepares to defend them.]

KAYA:
  Hold on!!

[Seeing that the girls are in danger, Ashitaka shoots an arrow through one of the
Tatari-gami's red "eyes." The monster stops; Ashitaka attempts to keep himself
and Yakkuru between the monster and the village while the girls flee.]

ASHITAKA:
  [To the girls]
  Hurry!

[Suddenly the Tatari-gami's tentacles explode into action. Although Yakkuru
gallops away from the Tatari-gami, Ashitaka's right arm gets caught by the
Tatari-gami's black feelers/snakes. Despite this, Ashitaka manages to shoot an
arrow through the Tatari-gami's forehead. The Tatari-gami collapses, and its black
"snakes" gradually disappear, revealing a huge Boar God underneath.]

MAN A:
  He beat it!!

KAYA:
  Ani-sama!

MAN C:
  Get Hii-sama, hurry!

MAN B:
  Don't let the fire go out!

[Ashitaka falls off Yakkuru in pain.]

KAYA:
  Ani-sama!

ASHITAKA:
  Kaya, don't touch.
  It's not an ordinary wound.

MAN A:
  Ashitaka is wounded! Where's Hii-sama?

HII:
  Everyone, you mustn't get any closer!

KAYA:
  Hii-sama!

HII:
  Pour this water slowly onto the wound.

KAYA:
  Yes ma'am.

HII:
  [To the boar]
  Though I know not from whence thou came, rampaging god,
  I would humbly and respectfully speak to thee.
  We will build a mound on this place, and observe a festival in honor of thy
  departed spirit.
  Forget thine anger, and be calmed.

BOAR:
  You damned filthy humans.
  You will know well my suffering and hatred......

[The boar's flesh dissolves.]

[That night in the village shrine, Hii, Ashitaka, and several of the village elders
are sitting. One wall of the shrine is a cliffside which bulges into the room; this
has become a shintai or "god-body" -- an object of worship in a Shinto shrine in
which the ghost of a deity is believed to dwell. Beneath the shintai is a small
altar flanked by flickering lamps. Hii and Ashitaka sit facing each other on either
side of the altar. Hii is fortune-telling by casting small pebbles on a cloth mat
in front of her; Ashitaka sits upright, hiding his resignation.]

HII:
  Well, this has become a worrisome situation.
  That boar turned up from a land in the far west.
  Driven mad by the poison of his mortal wounds,
  his flesh rotting away,
  he ran and ran towards here, accumulating curses,
  until finally he became a Tatari-gami.
  My dear Ashitaka-hiko...

ASHITAKA:
  Yes ma'am.

HII:
  Show everyone your right arm.

JII-JI:
  Hii-sama!

HII:
  My dear Ashitaka-hiko,
  are you prepared to gaze at your own destiny?

ASHITAKA:
  Yes ma'am.
  I decided the moment I shot my arrow at the Tatari-gami.

HII:
  Hmm...... that wound will eventually reach your bone,
  and probably will kill you.

YAMA-OJI:
  ...... Hii-sama, isn't there anything that we can do?

MAN A:
  Ashitaka saved our village and protected our girls!!

MAN B:
  To only sit and wait for death......

HII:
  No one can change destiny.
  However, it is up to him to decide whether to just wait, or to proceed for himself.
  Look...
  This is the thing that ate into that boar's body.
  It smashed his bones, tore up his guts, giving him cruel pain and suffering.
  Otherwise, would the boar have become the likes of a Tatari-gami?......
  There is something ominous happening in the western lands.
  Going there, to ascertain everything with unclouded eyes,
  you may possibly discover a way to sever that curse.

ASHITAKA:
  Yes ma'am.

OLD MAN:
  Defeated in the war against the Yamato regime,
  we have been hidden away in this place for more than 500 years.
  Now we've heard that the power of the Yamato king has withered, and that even the
  fangs of the Shoguns were broken.
  Yet, the bloodline of our clan also weakens.
  It may be unchangeable fate that, at this time, the young man who should have become
  our leader departs to the west.

[Ashitaka cuts off his mage -- the topknot of his hair -- and places it on the
shrine's altar.]

HII:
  According to our rules, we may not see you off.
  I wish you well.

[Ashitaka leaves the shrine, alone. Ashitaka prepares to leave the village, with
Yakkuru as his only companion.]

KAYA:
  Ani-sama!

ASHITAKA:
  Kaya, they forbid any farewells......

KAYA:
  I shall accept punishment.
  I beg you, please accept this gift as a substitute for my presence!

ASHITAKA:
  Isn't this your precious crystal knife?

KAYA:
  I blew a breath upon it to make a good-luck charm.
  I will always be thinking about you, always.
  Surely...... surely......

ASHITAKA:
  I will, also.
  I will always think of you, Kaya.

[Ashitaka rides quickly out of the village; Kaya stares after him.]

2. NEAR A MOUNTAIN VILLAGE AND A TOWN MARKET (CENTRAL JAPAN?)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Ashitaka continues his lonely journey. One day, he comes down from a mountain and
sees a group of villagers, including a strange-looking Buddhist priest or monk, being
attacked by a group of Samurai and soldiers.]

ASHITAKA:
  (A war......?)

ORDINARY SOLDIER A:
  You wretches!

ORDINARY SOLDIER C:
  Encircle them!

SOLDIER:
  [noticing Ashitaka]
  You bastard, it's a helmeted head!

[Archers on foot attack Ashitaka. Ashitaka and Yakkuru start to make a run for it.]

ORDINARY SOLDIERS:
  [Come back!]
  Fight! A contest!

[Ashitaka sees Ordinary Soldier B about to slaughter a woman.]

ASHITAKA:
  STOP IT!

[Ashitaka shoots an arrow through the hilt of the soldier's sword in order to knock
it out of the soldier's hands. In the instant Ashitaka shoots, the mark on his right
arm aches violently, as if the Tatari-gami's "snakes" are still on his arm. He
shoots the arrow with such strength that it rips the arms of the soldier off,
pinning them to a tree.]

ORDINARY SOLDIER B:
  Huh......?!
  Ugh!

ASHITAKA:
  (What the hell is up with this arm?!)

MOUNTED ARCHER:
  He won't get away, I see him!

ASHITAKA:
  I'm coming through, don't hinder me!!

[Another of Ashitaka's arrows decapitates the Mounted Archer. Ashitaka and Yakkuru
disappear into the forest.]

SAMURAI:
  It's a demon......

[After the incident, Ashitaka washes his right arm at a waterfall.]

ASHITAKA:
  The wound's becoming darker......

[Later, at a town market, the strange monk from the village battle is eating at a
market booth.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Oh damn, this tastes like hot water......

[He sees Ashitaka dealing with a rice merchant.]

  Oh, there he is!

ASHITAKA:
  Will this do?

RICE VENDOR WOMAN:
  What the hell is this?
  This isn't money.
  If you don't have money, then give back the rice.

JIKO-BOU:
  Wait, wait, let this monk take a look.
  Hmm......?! Oh!! This is......
  Woman, this is a sizable gold nugget!
  So, if you'd prefer cash, I'd pay the bill.
  [In return,] please hand over this.

CROWD:
  He said it was gold!
  Gold?!

JIKO-BOU:
  Everyone, is there a money house around here? Or not?
  As far as I can see, it's worth a bag of rice, no, three bags......

CROWD:
  Three bags?!
  Eh, enviable...

[In the confusion, Ashitaka disappears into the crowd.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Hey, wait a minute!

[Jiko-Bou makes to follow Ashitaka.]

RICE VENDOR WOMAN:
  Give that back to me, it's mine!

[Jiko-Bou catches up with Ashitaka outside the town.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Hey! So, not so fast!
  No need to thank me.
  I'm the one who wants to thank you.
  When I was dragged into that backwater samurai skirmish, I was rescued due to your
  assistance.
  My, you surely were just like a fierce god.

[Ashitaka gives Jiko-Bou a sharp look, then out of the corner of his eye sees three
suspicious-looking people from the town following them at a distance.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Hmm, so you noticed?
  It's what happens when you wave the likes of a gold nugget in people's faces.
  Indeed, people's hearts harden and become rough like hemp.
  It'd be rather boring if we're attacked in our sleep.
  Shall we run?...... Eh?

[That night, near the ruins of a village, Jiko-Bou is cooking Ashitaka's rice.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Hmm...... so a boar became a Tatari-gami......

ASHITAKA:
  I followed his footprints,
  but just as I descended to the village, I lost him.

JIKO-BOU:
  That's to be expected.
  Look over there.
  There used to be a village the last time I visited.
  Floods, mudslides...... no doubt, many people must have died.
  War, poverty, sickness, starvation;
  the human world is crowded with the dead, who died swallowing their resentment.
  If you're talking about a curse, then this world is a curse in itself.

  [Tastes his cooking]
  Ummm, yeah, delicious.

ASHITAKA:
  I made a mistake going down to the village.
  I murdered two people in the process.

JIKO-BOU:
  No, because of you I was saved.
  Bring out your bowl.
  You must eat first.
  People die anyhow.
  It just comes sooner or later.
  Hmm, what an elegant bowl.
  Looking at you, it reminds me of a people of ancient times, as passed down in old
  writings.
  The heroic Emishi clan said to exist in the far Eastern lands, sitting astride
  Akashishi, using arrowheads of stone......

[Ashitaka gives Jiko-Bou a searching look, then looks away.]

JIKO-BOU:
  The essential thing is to not be consumed by death.
  Well, that was something I learned from my teacher.
  Go on, it's your rice.
  Eat up.

ASHITAKA:
  Have you ever seen anything like this?

JIKO-BOU:
  ...... This is...?

ASHITAKA:
  It came from within the boar's body.
  It is what gave the gigantic boar the deadly wound.

JIKO-BOU:
  As you advance further and further west from here,
  within the mountain recesses, there is a deep forest that bars humans.
  It is the forest of the Shishi-gami.

ASHITAKA:
  The forest of the Shishi-gami......

JIKO-BOU:
  I've heard that the animals that inhabit the forest are all enormous, and exist as
  they were in ancient times.

[Early the next morning, Ashitaka leaves for the West, alone again.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Just as I expected, he's going......

3. MOUNTAINS NEAR TATARA BA, WESTERN JAPAN (IZUMO?)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[In the rain, Lady Eboshi and her men are transporting food to Tatara Ba. A long
train of cows carrying heavy burdens is slowly proceeding a narrow mountain road
above a ravine.]

MEN:
  Go, go!
  Move!

EBOSHI:
  Everyone, it's only a little further.
  Don't let your guard down!

WATCHMAN:
  Here they come!
  It's the Wolf Gods!

[Two large wolves -- Yama-inu, or "Mountain Dogs" -- attack the train. A masked
human is riding one of the wolves.]

EBOSHI:
  Calm down the cattle.
  Keep cool, put the formations together.

GONZA:
  Don't let the gun powder get wet by being in a hurry.
  Draw them in close!

EBOSHI:
  Number one, fire!

[Ashitaka, riding Yakkuru through the forest near the ravine, hears sounds like
thunder coming from the mountain. Meanwhile, on the mountain path, the wolves come
around for another attack.]

EBOSHI:
  Number two, fire!

[The wolves retreat.]

GONZA:
  Damned monsters, their bark is worse than their bite.

EBOSHI:
  Those are just the pups.
  Why does the mother not come......

[A huge wolf with a forked tail attacks. Several cattle and men fall into the
ravine.]

EBOSHI:
  It's Moro!!
  Come, Moro!!

[Eboshi shoots Moro with an Ishibiya ("stone fire arrow," or hand cannon of Chinese
origin). Moro falls into the ravine.]

GONZA:
  We killed her!!

EBOSHI:
  She is immortal.
  She won't die from something like this.

GONZA:
  We also took a pretty bad beating.

EBOSHI:
  We leave immediately.

GONZA:
  What shall we do about those that fell into the ravine?

EBOSHI:
  Re-form the ranks!!


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. A RIVER NEAR THE FOREST OF THE SHISHI-GAMI
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Ashitaka is crossing a river in the bottom of the ravine when he finds two wounded
men.]

ASHITAKA:
  He's breathing!!
  Hold on!!

[Yakkuru and Ashitaka sense that someone else is nearby. Peering through the woods,
he spots a huge white wolf and a girl on the other side of the river. The girl is
attending to the wolf's wound by sucking out the blood. Ashitaka shouts across the
river to them.]

ASHITAKA:
  My name is Ashitaka.
  I came here from the faraway lands to the east.
  Are you the old gods I have heard inhabit the forest of the Shishi-gami?!

[The girl gives him back only a cold stare, and disappears into the forest with
the wolves.]

SAN:
  [looking at Ashitaka]
  Leave!!


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. IN THE FOREST OF THE SHISHI-GAMI
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[One of the two wounded men screams. Ashitaka hurries back to find him staring in
fright at a small, white-green, semi-transparent being that is sitting nearby.]

ASHITAKA:
  Kodama......? There are Kodama here, too?
  Quiet. If you move you'll worsen your wounds!
  If you let it do as it likes, no harm will come to you.
  It's a sign that the forest is plentiful.

KOUROKU:
  These things call the Shishi-gami.

ASHITAKA:
  Shishi-gami? A large mountain dog?

KOUROKU:
  No. Something even more frightening, the chief of the monsters!
  Aa, it vanished!

ASHITAKA:
  Yakkuru is unconcerned.
  There is no danger nearby.

  [to a Kodama ("tree spirit") sitting on Yakkuru's back]
  I'm sorry, but with your permission we will pass through your forest.

[Yakkuru carries Kouroku, while Ashitaka carries the other man on his back.]

KOUROKU:
  ...... I beg of you, let's go back!
  There's a path on the other side of the river.
  It's impossible to escape this forest!

ASHITAKA:
  The river's current is too strong for us to cross.
  Besides, if we don't hurry, it will be too late for this wounded man.

[As they walk through the forest, many Kodama appear in front of them.]

ASHITAKA:
  Huh. Are you guiding us?
  Or trying to make us lose our way?......

KOUROKU:
  Sir, these things don't want us to return home!
  More and more keep appearing.

ASHITAKA:
  [to the Kodama]
  So that's your mother?
  What a splendid tree!

[They come to a pond in the middle of the forest.]

ASHITAKA:
  That girl's footprints, and the mountain dogs' as well.
  Can this be their domain?......

KOUROKU:
  Sir, now for sure it's dangerous!
  The entrance to the other world is here.

ASHITAKA:
  It looks like it, doesn't it? Let's rest a while.

[Ashitaka puts down the wounded man he was carrying, and goes to get some water from
the pond. He sees a number of butterflies hovering over some marks in the grass.]

ASHITAKA:
  A footprint......?
  Three hoof marks......
  Still fresh...

[On the other side of the pond, Ashitaka sees a herd of deer. At the moment he sees
a strange beast which looks like a buck with many antlers, his right arm starts
shaking violently, causing him great pain.]

KOUROKU:
  S-sir, what's the matter?!

[As the mysterious beast disappears into the forest, Ashitaka's pain recedes.]

KOUROKU:
  Sir, are you all right?
  Your face is as white as a sheet!
  That's why I told you it's dangerous.

ASHITAKA:
  Did you see anything?

KOUROKU:
  Huh......?

ASHITAKA:
  No, never mind......

[Ashitaka gives some water to the other wounded man, Yashichi.]

ASHITAKA:
  Only a little more to endure. Hang in there.

YASHICHI:
  ......thanks......

ASHITAKA:
  [to himself, looking towards the spot where the mysterious beast appeared]
  (...... He's gone.)

[They resume their journey through the forest.]

ASHITAKA:
  (How is it...... Since leaving that place, suddenly this man's body became
  lighter.)

KOUROKU:
  [tries his broken arm]
  Huh? It doesn't hurt!
  It's healed!!
  Ouch ouch ouch, like I thought, it's broken...

6. TATARA BA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Ashitaka and the two wounded men emerge from the forest in sight of Tatara Ba,
which is a huge island fortress on the other side of a lake. Smoke is rising from
the center of it, and the mountains around it are totally devoid of trees.]

KOUROKU:
  Ahhh. Sir, wonderful, you're right!
  We've arrived at the Tatara!

ASHITAKA:
  Seems almost like a castle.

KOUROKU:
  It's Eboshi-sama's huge tatara.
  It heats iron sand and produces iron.

[Ashitaka pulls the mask of his hood over his face. Two men of Tatara Ba, carriers
waiting by their ferry boat on Ashitaka's side of the lake, see Ashitaka's party
approach.]

COOLIE I:
  People are coming from the forest.

COOLIE II:
  Mononoke......?!

KOUROKU:
  Hey, it's me, Kouroku, the cattle herdsman!

[Within Tatara Ba, Kouroku's co-workers receive the news of his return.]

CATTLE HERDSMEN:
  What, Kouroku...
  You're sure it's not a ghost?
  You're kidding......
  Phenomenal!!
  Notify his wife!

[A number of Ishibiya troops are lounging about and eating when the news about the
arrival of their comrade arrives.]

ISHIBIYA TROOPER:
  No lie.
  They're heading this way by boat right now!

ISHIBIYA TROOPS:
  Did you see his face for certain?

GONZA:
  Eh, what is it?!
  I'm writing, be quiet now!

CATTLE HERDSMAN:
  Kouroku, who we thought had died, just left the opposite shore!

GONZA:
  What?!

[The boat reaches the shore.]

CATTLE HERDSMAN I:
  You're not a ghost, are you?

CATTLE HERDSMAN II:
  What about the others?

CATTLE HERDSMAN III:
  There were two other men that fell!

KOUROKU:
  ......We were the only ones who were saved.

[A number of people watch the spectacle from the gate of Tatara Ba, which is at
the top of a bluff facing the lake shore.]

WOMAN:
  The ones that die are always the cattle herdsmen...

[Gonza comes out of the gate and pauses momentarily, observing the situation
below.]

GONZA:
  Clear the way!

MAN:
  Lord Gonza, who might that hooded one be?

GONZA:
  I haven't seen the likes of him before...

[At the shore, Ishibiya troops lift their wounded comrade out of the boat, while
the cattle herdsmen handle Kouroku.]

KOUROKU:
  Ishibiya troops! This man carried your comrade all the way here.
  Thank him!
  ......Aa, ow ow!
  ......Don't hold me there!
  ......Aa ow, aa!

[One of the Ishibiya troops bows to Ashitaka. This infuriates Gonza, who stomps
down to face Ashitaka.]

GONZA:
  Sheesh.......!! You there, wait!!
  Firstly, I thank you for bringing us our wounded.
  But I'm not satisfied with the situation.
  You came within an hour after we arrived here.
  Furthermore, they say you carried a large man from the bottom of the valley,
  and came out of the Shishi-gami's forest......

[A woman comes running from the gate of Tatara Ba down to face Kouroku at the lake
shore.]

TOKI:
  Kouroku, you're alive?!

KOUROKU:
  Toki!

TOKI:
  A-a-a, you slouch!!
  A cattle herdsman breaking a leg, how are we supposed to eat?!

KOUROKU:
  But I couldn't help it......

TOKI:
  You make me worry all the time.
  You might as well have been eaten by the mountain dogs.
  Then I could find a better man!

KOUROKU:
  Toki, please forgive me!

GONZA:
  Toki, can't you have your domestic quarrels somewhere else?

TOKI:
  What makes you so great?
  Abandoning the wounded like that.
  What good are you as a guard?
  You haven't even pushed a tatara once in your life.
  You've got to risk your life in a crucial moment like that......

GONZA:
  It couldn't be helped...... Ah......

TOKI:
  [to Ashitaka]
  Thank you. I'm glad you saved even that sort of husband!

ASHITAKA:
  That's good. I was starting to get worried that maybe I shouldn't have brought
  him with me.

TOKI:
  Eh?!...... Hahahaha...
  Hey, you must be a good-looking man.
  Please let me see your face!

[Lady Eboshi appears with a group of people near the gate above them.]

EBOSHI:
  Gonza...
  I'd like to thank him later on; guide our guest.
  Kouroku...

KOUROKU:
  Y-yes...

EBOSHI:
  It's good you came back safely. I'm sorry.

KOUROKU:
  Y-yes...

TOKI:
  That's absurd, Eboshi-sama.
  The fool is just spoiled!

EBOSHI:
  Toki, please forgive me as well.
  It was shameful since I was with him when this thing happened.

TOKI:
  Oh no, if it were just the men, about this time they'd all be settling down in
  peace and harmony inside a mountain dog's belly!

WOMEN WITH EBOSHI:
  Hahaha......

WOMAN I:
  Really!

WOMAN II:
  No mistake there!

EBOSHI:
  Traveling gentleman, be at ease and rest well!

[Ashitaka lowers his mask and bows his head towards the departing Lady Eboshi.]

TOKI:
  Oh!! I knew it, you are a good-looking man!

[That evening, after the gates of Tatara Ba are closed, the rice brought by Lady
Eboshi's caravan is being distributed to the townspeople.]

MAN:
  This is the rice we defeated Moro to bring here, eat it gratefully!

WOMAN:
  Hurry up!

[Ashitaka is eating with the cattle herdsmen. A number of women are gathered outside
the door of the dining hall, peering within.]

WOMEN:
  Hahaha,
  Where, where?
  Eh, that man?

WOMAN I:
  Really, just like Toki said!

WOMAN II:
  A good-looking man, isn't he?

WOMAN III:
  Isn't he a little young?

WOMAN I:
  Even though you don't care about stuff like a man's age...

CATTLE HERDSMAN A:
  Be quiet, won't you?
  We're holding a wake for the dead here!

CATTLE HERDSMAN B:
  As for good-looking men, here are some, too!

WOMAN I:
  Not me. What, cattle herdsmen?!

WOMAN III:
  Hey, traveling gentleman. Why don't you come to our place?
  Don't stay in a stinking animal pen like this.

CATTLE HERDSMAN C:
  What are you talking about?
  We put our lives on the line to bring you the rice you're eating!
  I hope your mouth rots off.

WOMAN II:
  Huh, who makes the iron that buys that rice?!

WOMAN III:
  We tread the tatara all night long!

ASHITAKA:
  If you like, please show me your workplace.

WOMAN I:

  The Shishi-gami both gives and takes life.

LARGE BOAR:

MORO:
  It is because he feared death. As I do now......

SAN:
  [staring at Moro]
  ......!?

MORO:

ASHITAKA:

  Out of necessity I killed a Tatari-gami that attacked my village.
  It was a large Boar God. This is my proof.

[Ashitaka displays his right hand, bearing the mark of the Tatari-gami's curse.

SAN:
  ......!!

ASHITAKA:

  However, the Shishi-gami healed my wound, but did not erase the mark of my curse.

MORO:
  It's Okkotonushi. At last someone has come who might understand a little.

SAN:
  Wait, Lord Okkotonushi!

OKKOTONUSHI:

SAN:
  Your eyes......

OKKOTONUSHI:
  Thank you, young one.......
  It is a sad thing that a Tatari-gami emerged from our clan.

ASHITAKA:
  Lord Okkotonushi, is there any way to erase this curse?......

OKKOTONUSHI:

quickly on the water until it is out of sight.]

SAN:
  ......!! Shishi-gami-sama...

10. MORO'S CAVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Ashitaka is awakened by the pain of his cursed arm to find himself lying in a
cave. San is sleeping beside him.]

SONG (BACKGROUND MUSIC):
  The trembling string of a taut bow --
  Your heart is unsettled by the moonlight --
  The beauty of a sharpened blade --
  Your silhouette, like its keen point --
  The sorrow and anguish
  that your naked heart hides behind --
  Only the spirits of the forest know --
  Only the Mononoke.

[The pain of his curse returns; Ashitaka walks out onto a ledge in front of the
cave entrance, and looks down at the forest under the moonlight. Moro is lying atop
the cave entrance.]

MORO:
  Do you suffer?
  If you jump now, from here, you could end it all.
  As you regain your strength, so will your wound spread.

ASHITAKA:
  It seems I was asleep for many days.
  All I remember was being in the care of that young girl.

MORO:
  If you had raised even a single cry, I would have ripped your throat apart. It
  seems I missed my chance.
  What is it you stare at?

ASHITAKA:
  It's such a beautiful forest.
  Has Okkotonushi moved yet?

MORO:
  Go back inside the cave, young one.
  How can you hear the sorrowful cries of the forest as the boars move among the
  trees?
  I sit here listening to the forest's desperate cries with my dying body while
  waiting for that woman to appear!
  How I dream of the moment when I can sink my fangs into her head!

ASHITAKA:
  Moro, is there any way that the forest and humans can coexist without conflict?
  Is it really true that there is no other way?!

MORO:
  The humans but grow and grow in numbers.
  In time they will reach even here.

ASHITAKA:
  What do you intend to do with San?
  Do you intend to abandon her as well?

MORO:
  Just like a human. Such an irrational, selfish thought.
  San is one of us. She lives with the forest, and shall die when it dies.

ASHITAKA:
  Release the girl! She is human!

MORO:
  Quiet, young one!
  How can you pretend to understand that girl's misfortune?
  She was but a child who was thrown to us so that the humans could escape our
  teeth!
  Unable to become completely human or Mountain Dog...
  she is my dear, ugly, furless child!
  And you say that you can save San?!

ASHITAKA:
  I don't know.
  However, I know we can live together.

MORO:
  How would you live together?
  Would you fight the humans with San at your side?

ASHITAKA:
  No! That would only increase the hatred and grief!

MORO:
  Young one, there is nothing that you can do.
  You will eventually be consumed by the curse and die. Leave by the dawning of
  the sun!

[Ashitaka reenters the cave to find San awake.]

SAN:
  Were you able to walk?

ASHITAKA:
  Thank you. It is due to you and the Shishi-gami.

[San smiles, and goes back to sleep. Ashitaka watches her for a while, and goes
to sleep. The next morning, when Ashitaka wakes up, San and the wolves are already
gone. His belongings are neatly laid near him, together with some wrapped food.
Ashitaka understands that this is a message from San for him to leave the mountain.
Yakkuru is waiting for him outside the cave.]

ASHITAKA:
  Yakkuru! Sorry to have worried you!

[He attempts to jump down to Yakkuru, but falls.]

  My leg's become so weak...

[When Ashitaka mounts Yakkuru, one of Moro's two sons appears, and leads them down
the mountain through the forest.]

  It's too quiet. All the Kodama have disappeared.

[They reach a streambed filled with huge boulders.]

  The air smells of the village of Tatara Ba, we're close.

[The wolf stops leading them. Ashitaka considers a moment, then pulls out the
crystal dagger given him by Kaya when he left the Emishi village.]

  Thank you for your guidance! I have one favor to ask you!
  Please give this to San!

[Ashitaka throws the dagger to the wolf, which catches it in its teeth and
disappears.]

  Everyone, together at once!

[Yakkuru approaches the freed wolf, and they stare at each other as if to
communicate.]

ASHITAKA:
  Everyone go down the hill and hide in the lake.

GATEKEEPER:
  Please be careful. The Ishibiya troops are also their allies now.

ASHITAKA:
  [handing over his bow]
  Please keep this for me; my last arrow broke.

  [to Yakkuru]
  You stay here with everyone else...

  [to the Tatara men]
  Take care of Yakkuru!

  [to the wolf]
  Quickly, to San! Eboshi will be there as well!

[Ashitaka and the wolf run into the forest.]

  [to Okkotonushi]

  [to the wolf]

  [to Okkotonushi]

WOLF:

SHOUJOU:

SHOUJOU II:
  This forest is no more!

SAN:

SHOUJOU II:
  Something neither human nor beast.

SAN:
  Neither human nor beast...

SHOUJOU:
  They come!

SHOUJOU II:
  The forest is done for!

SAN:
  The warriors...

OKKOTONUSHI:
  They're back... they've come back.
  My warriors have come back for me!
  Come, let us go forth to the Shishi-gami!

[Okkotonushi starts charging towards the Shishi-gami's pond, the "ghost" boars in

SAN:
  Okkotonushi-sama, calm yourself! Those who die can never come back!

OKKOTONUSHI:
  Shishi-gami-sama! Come forth!

SAN:
  Okkotonushi-sama, please, calm yourself!

WOLF:
  He's done for! Leave him behind.

SAN:
  No, we can't leave him like this! He'll become a Tatari-gami!
  You must go back and tell mother about the humans' trap!
  If mother comes, she will lend us her strength!
  Hurry, go, you must not let the Moro clan die!
  Go.

[The wolf leaps over the boarhide-covered humans and disappears. Okkotonushi

SAN:
  I will kill the first one who comes forth.
  I will expose your true form for all the forest to see!

[San hears a wolf's call in the distance.]

  Ashitaka?

[The "ghost" boars approach Okkotonushi again.]

OKKOTONUSHI:
  Hot, so hot.

SAN:
  No! Okkotonushi-sama, you mustn't become a Tatari-gami!

[San desperately attempts to clear the "snakes" from Okkotonushi, but they keep
growing.]

SAN:
  Okkotonushi-sama...

[One of the men in the boar hides slings a stone at San, knocking her unconscious.
On a clifftop, Ashitaka hears the answering howling from the forest.]

ASHITAKA:
  He answered!
  Did you get that?

WOLF:
  San is in danger.

ASHITAKA:
  Let's go!

[They hurry down the cliff to the forest. San regains consciousness to find herself
caught in the black feelers of Okkotonushi. She sees that the black feelers are also
coming out of herself.]

SAN:
  It burns...
  No! I don't want to become a Tatari-gami! Okkotonushi-sama!

[Ashitaka and the wolf approach a sheer cliff face.]

WOLF:
  Too slow, get on!

[Ashitaka jumps onto the wolf's back, and they fly down the cliff. At the bottom,
they come across the party of Lady Eboshi and Jiko-Bou advancing in the forest.]

ASHITAKA:
  Eboshi...

[Jiko-Bou's men fire Ishibiya at the wolf.]

ASHITAKA:
  [to the wolf]
  Shit! You go on ahead!

  [jumps off the wolf's back at a distance from Lady Eboshi]
  Eboshi! Listen to me!

JIKO-BOU:
  [to the Ishibiya troops]
  Stop, stop!

EBOSHI:
  Ashitaka, is that you?

ASHITAKA:
  Tatara Ba is being attacked by Lord Asano!
  Stop this futile fight against the Shishi-gami and return at once!
  The women are fighting.
  The men are hiding down from the lake.
  Everyone is waiting for your return!

EBOSHI:
  What proof do you have?

ASHITAKA:
  None!
  If I could, I would have stayed and fought for Tatara Ba.

EBOSHI:
  Are you telling me to not kill the Shishi-gami, and instead kill samurai?

ASHITAKA:
  No! Is there no way the forest and Tatara Ba can coexist?

[Ashitaka disappears into the forest.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Which side is he on?

GONZA:
  Eboshi-sama, let's go back.

EBOSHI:
  I gave the women enough arms to hold out for a while yet.
  And as I told them, "One's life is but for one to protect."
  It's a pond, the Shishi-gami is close.

JIKO-BOU:
  [to the Karakasa-ren]
  We're almost there. Don't let your guard down.

KARAKASA-REN:

JIKO-BOU:

15. SHISHI-GAMI'S POND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[Ashitaka arrives at the Shishi-gami's pond and finds Moro lying on the shore.]

ASHITAKA:
  Moro, are you dead?
  San! Where are you?
  San!

[Swallowed up by the black feelers of the Tatari-gami, San faintly hears Ashitaka.]

SAN:
  Ashitaka!

[The Tatari/Okkotonushi comes out of the forest towards the pond, followed by the
hunters wearing boar hides.]

ASHITAKA:
  Okkotonushi...

HUNTER:
  Move aside.

ASHITAKA:
  If you fight here, the Shishi-gami will not come out.
  Okkotonushi, please calm down!
  Okkotonushi, please give back the Princess of the Mountain Dogs.
  Where is San?
  San, can you hear me? It's Ashitaka!

[Ashitaka sees San's leg sticking out of the black feelers.]

  San...

[Ashitaka dashes towards Okkotonushi to save her, breaking through the encircling
"ghost" boars.]

HUNTER:
  Get rid of him.
  Kill him, kill him!

[The Karakasa-ren hunters shoot darts at Ashitaka, but Moro's two wolf sons attack
the hunters. Ashitaka jumps into the black mass of Tatari/Okkotonushi.]

ASHITAKA:
  San!
  San...

[Ashitaka and San try to reach for each other.]

SAN:
  Ashitaka...

[Ashitaka is thrown into the air as San is swallowed more deeply into
Tatari/Okkotonushi. Ashitaka bounces off the prone Moro's head, and falls into the
pond, unconscious.]

SAN:
  Ashitaka!

[Moro opens her eyes slowly.]

MORO:
  And I thought I was saving the last of my strength for that woman...

[Moro raises herself slowly on her front legs, the rear part of her body still
immersed in the Shishi-gami's pond.]

HUNTER:
  Set up a defensive line!

MORO:
  [to her two wolf sons]
  Do not interfere.
  The Tatari-gami's curse is not yours to bear.

[As the young wolves back away, Tatari/Okkotonushi approaches Moro.]

  Huh, you've even lost your power of speech?

[Jiko-Bou, Lady Eboshi, and their party are hiding behind trees near the pond.]

JIKO-BOU:
  [to the hunters]
  You did well.
  Help the wounded. Go.
  My, my, what a scary sight indeed.

[The Shishi-gami appears on the distant island in the middle of the pond.]

  There it is.

[Tatari/Okkotonushi butts Moro with his snout.]

MORO:
  Give me back my daughter.

[Moro uses her jaws to extract San from the black snakes/feelers.]

  Ashitaka, can you save San?

[At the bottom of the pond, half-unconscious, Ashitaka hears Moro. Ashitaka opens
his eyes, and swims to the surface of the pond. When he surfaces, Ashitaka and
Tatari/Okkotonushi see the Shishi-gami slowly walking on the water towards them.
Tatari/Okkotonushi becomes still as it stares at the Shishi-gami.]

ASHITAKA:
  Shishi-gami...

[As the Shishi-gami reaches Ashitaka, Eboshi, hiding behind the trees, shoots the
Shishi-gami. The bullet passes through the Shishi-gami's head. The Shishi-gami's
hooves start to sink into the water.]

ASHITAKA:
  Eboshi! Don't shoot!

[The Shishi-gami regains its balance, and continues walking on the water past
Ashitaka as if nothing had happened.]

[The Shishi-gami reaches the shore, and approaches Okkotonushi.]

JIKO-BOU:
  The Ishibiya didn't work.

EBOSHI:

[Ashitaka jumps out of the pond, receives San from Moro, and jumps back into the
pond with San in his arms.]

ASHITAKA:
  San!
  You mustn't die.

[Ashitaka pulls San under the surface of the pond with him; the water of the pond

kisses Okkotonushi's muzzle. Okkotonushi collapses and dies. Moro, standing next
to him, collapses at the same time.]

JIKO-BOU:
  The Shishi-gami absorbs life?!
  My god!

[As Ashitaka returns to the surface with San, the Shishi-gami looks up at the moon

JIKO-BOU:
  Oh, no, he's turning into the Didarabotchi!

EBOSHI:

EBOSHI:

  It will take your life.
  Catch! The promised head.

GONZA:

  [to his men carrying the container]

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[The siege has temporarily quieted as the Samurai take a night rest. Inside the

  That's fine. You live in the forest; I will live in Tatara Ba.

  I'm so ashamed. Being saved on the back of a Mountain Dog.

  Everyone, let's start over. We'll make this a good village.

[Jiko-Bou observes this from his boulder.]

JIKO-BOU:
  Oh man, I can't win against fools.

[At the shore of the Shishi-gami's pond, great dead trees look like white bones. On
the foot of them are sprouts, and a little Kodama appears among them. As it clatters
its head, the sound echoes through the forest...]

[Director's Note by Hayao Miyazaki, included in the original annotated script:]

"There cannot be a happy ending to the fight between the raging gods and humans.
However, even in the middle of hatred and killings, there are things worth living
for. A wonderful meeting, or a beautiful thing can exist. We depict hatred, but it
is to depict that there are more important things. We depict a curse, to depict
the joy of liberation.

What we should depict is, how the boy understands the girl, and the process in
which the girl opens her heart to the boy.

At the end, the girl will say to the boy, 'I love you, Ashitaka. But I cannot
forgive humans.'

Smiling, the boy should say, 'That is fine. Live with me.'"
